Cardi B Accused Of Setting Up Akbar V Fight, GloRilla Catches Stray

Akbar V was recently attacked while in New York City, and she believes that Cardi B set it up. The Love & Hip-Hop actress explained her thoughts on social media and threw shade at GloRilla for making light of the situation.

Per a report from a gossip account on X (formerly Twitter), V was at Agenda lounge on Monday (April 29) and got hit from behind by multiple people before falling to the ground. Additional footage that surfaced online shows her being stomped while on the ground before security helped her up.

A nearby witness said that the attacker fled the scene and ran down the block. The alleged attacker was identified as a close friend of Star Brim and is followed on social media by Cardi B, leading Akbar V to believe that this is no coincidence.

“Aye tell her blood big [homie] she pu**y cause she [haul] a** and ran to the nearest exit and down the block,” Akbar V wrote on X. “[That sh*t] ain’t respected yall [sent] the neighborhood crack head.”

She shared a video of the alleged attacked and threw more insults her way. “And look at this grandma face looking slow looking a** [h**] here [bi**h lol,” she wrote. “Yall h**s are [ugly as f**k] this [why] i know it was Cardi who sent this lil dirty bum washed up [h**] *laughing emojis* didn’t even buy the [h**] some shoes [bi**h] had on sketchers Llh.”

GloRilla quoted the video of Akbar V getting stomped and asked, “Did da girl have on shell toes” in jest. V didn’t find that funny, and tweeted, “Bi**h ur head look like it bout to pop u slow looking a** lizard a** [h**].” Gloss Up quoted the same video with multiple laughing emojis, and V had smoke for her, too. “U laughing i can’t wait to beat the black off u [h**],” she wrote with laughing emojis.

Cardi B and Akbar V having tension isn’t new. In 2022, they traded subliminal shots over social media before things escalated into a direct feud that involved Offset. The Bronx rapper and GloRilla celebrate the success of their “Tomorrow 2” music video, but V was apprehensive about whether the claim that they got six million views in three days was true or not.

“If @chartdata ain’t say it we ain’t believing the CAP,” she tweeted. Cardi countered and questioned if Chart Data had ever posted Akbar V at all. “I don’t really like the internet games,” she added. “My dms is open and also the streets!”

V followed up by sharing screenshots of texts with the “Up” rapper, which led to the Grammy winner doubling down on taking it off the internet and into real life. “If we said what we said on the phone why keep texting? These all these bi**hes want …INTERNET SHOWS..I’m done,” she said.

It wouldn’t be a Cardi B feud without someone bringing up her longtime foe Nicki Minaj, and Akbar V did just that. “Bi**h talking bout she can change my life,” she wrote on X. “[H**] @NICKIMINAJ change my life I’m on a tape u produced and didn’t know u produced it and showed love to ur dog face a** still cause we was cordial and u ain’t take me number #1 so if u couldn’t change my life with that u can’t do it now.”

Needless to say, their hatred runs deep. Akbar V’s belief that Cardi ordered someone else to beat her up makes sense simply because of their tension, but may fall flat given the fact she offered her the fade directly in the past.
